Why india lost WTC 2021?

The ICC World Test Championship is a premier international cricket tournament held to determine the top Test team in the world. Spanning from 2019 to 2021, this championship brought together the finest cricketing nations in a thrilling battle for supremacy. In this blog post, we will take a closer look at the key highlights and memorable moments of the tournament.

  • Pitch Characteristics, Weather and Climate, Ball Behavior, Outfield and Fielding Conditions

    Pitch in India: In India, pitches tend to be dry, especially during the later stages of a Test match. The soil composition, influenced by subcontinent conditions, often leads to spin-friendly pitches that offer turn and variable bounce. The ball can grip the surface, assisting spin bowlers.
    Pitch in England: In England, pitches are generally more conducive to seam and swing bowling. The cooler and moister climate helps retain moisture in the pitches, allowing the ball to seam and swing, particularly in the initial stages of a Test match.

    India Weather and Climate: India has a tropical climate with hot and dry conditions prevailing in many regions. The weather can be extremely hot and humid during certain months, which affects player endurance and may impact the behavior of the pitch.
    England Weather and Climate: England has a temperate maritime climate, characterized by cooler temperatures and higher chances of rainfall. The weather can be unpredictable, and rain interruptions can affect the outcome of matches.

    Ball Behavior in India: In India, the dry conditions and spin-friendly pitches often result in significant turn for spin bowlers. The ball can grip the surface and spin sharply, making it challenging for batsmen to play spin bowling effectively.
    Ball Behavior England: In England, the cooler and moister conditions can assist seam bowlers, allowing the ball to seam and swing in the air. Batsmen need to adjust their techniques to cope with the lateral movement generated by the bowlers.

    Outfield and Fielding Conditions in India: Outfields in India tend to be dry and slower due to the drier climate. The ball may not travel as fast, affecting boundary scoring. The dry conditions can also result in dusty and abrasive fielding conditions.
    Outfield and Fielding Conditions in England: Outfields in England are often lush and well-maintained, allowing the ball to travel faster. The cooler climate can provide favorable fielding conditions, with softer and less abrasive surfaces.

    It's important to note that these differences are generalizations, and specific venues within India and England can have their own unique characteristics. Additionally, both countries host a variety of pitches

    Short of match practice in England weather conditions:

    it's true that the Indian team had limited match practice in England leading up to the WTC final due to the COVID-19 pandemic and other logistical challenges, it would be an oversimplification to attribute their loss solely to this factor. The New Zealand team, India's opponent in the final, also faced similar challenges and had their own preparations.
    Cricket matches are complex and involve various elements, including skills, tactics, form, and performance on the given day. While familiarity with conditions can play a role, it is ultimately the performance and execution of skills that determine the outcome.
