WTC 2019-2021

NewZealand beats india to win the first WTC trophy.

The ICC World Test Championship is a premier international cricket tournament held to determine the top Test team in the world. Spanning from 2019 to 2021, this championship brought together the finest cricketing nations in a thrilling battle for supremacy. In this blog post, we will take a closer look at the key highlights and memorable moments of the tournament.

  • Format and Structure:

    The ICC World Test Championship featured nine of the top Test-playing nations, namely Australia, Bangladesh, England, India, New Zealand, Pakistan, South Africa, Sri Lanka, and the West Indies. The teams competed in a two-year cycle of bilateral Test series, accumulating points based on their performance. Each series carried a specific weightage of points, adding a strategic element to the competition.

    Top Contenders:

    Throughout the championship, India emerged as a dominant force, showcasing exceptional skill and consistency. Led by the charismatic captain Virat Kohli, the Indian team displayed a blend of strong batting line-up, potent bowling attack, and sharp fielding prowess.

    Memorable Matches:

    Several matches in the ICC World Test Championship captivated fans worldwide. One of the standout encounters was the 2019 Ashes series between England and Australia. The series featured thrilling contests, including Ben Stokes' heroic innings at Headingley that secured a memorable victory for England.

    Another notable match was the India vs. Australia Border-Gavaskar series in 2020-2021. Despite injuries to key players, the Indian team, under the captaincy of Ajinkya Rahane, displayed immense resilience and emerged victorious, scripting a remarkable comeback in the series.

    The Final Showdown:

    After a fierce battle between the top teams, New Zealand and India secured their places in the final of the ICC World Test Championship. The highly anticipated match took place at the iconic Ageas Bowl in Southampton, England.
